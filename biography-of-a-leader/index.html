Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile man of India & Former President

Introduction

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

He was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th former president of india(2002-2007).

He is popularly known as the Missile Man of India Because of his missile and space technology.

Early life & Education

  • Worked as a Key Scientist in ISRO and DRDO.
  • Lead role in India's First Satellite Lauch vehicle (SLV - III).
  • Major Contributor to Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Received Bharat Ratna in 1997.
  • Played a Significant Role in 1998 Pokhran Nuclear Tests.

Famous Books

  • Wings Of Fire
  • Ignited Minds
  • India 2020

Inspiration

Dr.Kalam inspires millions of young people . He always said,

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Conclusion

He Passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. His Life continues to inspire everyone to work hard and serve the nation.

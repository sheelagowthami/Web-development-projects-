Featured Heritage Sites

Discover India's most iconic monuments with AI-powered explanations

Agra, Uttar Pradesh | 17th Century

Taj Mahal

The iconic white marble mausoleum, built by Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. A masterpiece of Mughal architecture and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Delhi | 17th Century

Red Fort

Historic fortified palace built by Shah Jahan. Constructed from vast slabs of red sandstone, it stands as a monumental symbol of sovereign Mughal power.

Hyderabad, Telangana | 16th Century

Charminar

An iconic mosque and monument featuring four grand minarets, built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to celebrate the end of a deadly plague.

Karnataka | 14th-16th Century

Hampi

The legendary sacred capital of the historical Vijayanagara Empire. Features captivating, vast ruins of sophisticated temples and structural marvels.

Delhi | 12th-13th Century

Qutub Minar

The tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 72.5 meters. Erected by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, it is an exquisite triumph of early Indo-Islamic engineering.

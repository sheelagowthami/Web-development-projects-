Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun,with a heavily cratered, rocky surface similar to Earth's Moon.

It is known for its extreme temperature fluctuations, the fastest orbit (88 Earth days), and a very slow rotation, which makes one solar day last longer than its year.

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun,known as the Red Planet ,due to iron oxide in its soil.It has a thin atmosphere of mostly carbon dioxide, a day that is about 24.6 hours long, and a year that is almost twice as long as an Earth year, at 687 Earth days.

Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the solar system, and has two small moons named Phobos and Deimos.

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system,a "gas giant" located fifth from the Sun. It is a world of extreme storms, including the Great Red Spot,

and is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium with a "stripy" appearance from swirling cloud bands. Jupiter also has a complex system of many moons and a powerful magnetic field.

ğŸª Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun,and the second-largest in our solar system. It is a gas giant known for its prominent rings, which are made of ice and rock particles. The planet is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium,

making it the least dense planet in the solar system, so much so that it would float in water if a large enough body of it existed. It has a fast rotation, with a day lasting just over 10 hours, and takes about 29.5 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

ğŸŒ€ Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun,known as an ice giant due to its composition of water, methane, and ammonia ices above a small, rocky core.

It is the third-largest planet in the solar system and is famous for its extreme axial tilt, causing it to rotate on its side. This tilt results in unique seasonal extremes, and the planet's blue-green color comes from the methane in its atmosphere absorbing red light.

ğŸŒŠ Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet,an ice giant known for its deep blue color, strong winds, and large storms.

It is similar to Uranus in composition, made of a thick atmosphere of hydrogen, helium, and methane, with a dense fluid of water, ammonia, and methane ice surrounding a rocky core. Neptune has 14 moons and faint rings and takes about 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun.

Apollo 11 was the NASA spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon, launching on July 16, 1969, and returning on July 24, 1969. Astronauts Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins were on the mission.

Armstrong and Aldrin became the first people to walk on the lunar surface, while Collins orbited above in the command module. The mission fulfilled a national goal set by President John F. Kennedy to land a man on the Moon and return him safely to Earth before the end of the decade.

Mangalyaan was India's first interplanetary mission, launched by ISRO in 2013 to orbit Mars and study its surface and atmosphere.
